About Anemia

What is Anemia?

Anemia is a condition in which the number of red blood cells or the hemoglobin concentration within them is lower than normal. It can cause fatigue, weakness, and other health complications.

Why Early Detection Matters

Early identification of the type of anemia helps initiate timely treatment, reducing the risk of severe health outcomes and improving quality of life.

Iron Deficiency Anemia

The most common type, caused by insufficient iron, leading to reduced hemoglobin production.

Vitamin B12 Deficiency

Occurs when the body lacks enough B12 to produce healthy red blood cells, often leading to megaloblastic anemia.

Sickle Cell Anemia

A genetic disorder causing misshapen red blood cells that block blood flow and cause pain and organ damage.

Thalassemia

An inherited blood disorder where the body makes an abnormal form or inadequate amount of hemoglobin.

Dimorphic Anemia

A condition where two types of anemia coexist, typically combining microcytic and macrocytic cells.
